OIL, ARMS & AMBITION
How three superpowers played the same crisis — very differently
🇺🇸 United States
"Our exports are booming!"
Energy Exporter · Arms Supplier · Dollar Safe Haven
The Shale Swagger
Energy Play
Elevated oil prices supercharged shale output. Export values surged in 2024 while allies paid premium for "friendly" barrels.
Windfall
Arms Economy
Defence contractors posted record order books. Every conflict dollar abroad recycled back into the US industrial base.
Export Boom
Hidden Cost
Domestic inflation stayed stubborn — fuel & food bills kept consumers squeezed even as Wall Street cheered a strong dollar.
Consumer Pain
🇷🇺 Russia
"We have leverage!"
Oil Revenue · Sanctions Pressure · Military Spending
The Sanction Gamble
Oil Revenues
High global prices propped up the treasury — but forced discounts to Asian buyers slashed real margins by nearly a third.
Price High, Margins Low
War Economy
Military spending consumed budget headroom. GDP looked positive on paper, but growth was entirely war-driven — not productive.
Hollow Growth
Ruble & Isolation
Cut off from Western finance, the rouble wobbled and import inflation bit hard. The nuclear arsenal became the only negotiation chip.
Structural Fragility
🇮🇳 India
"Buy low, sell refined!"
Strategic Buyer · Refinery Hub · Fastest GDP Growth
The Quiet Winner
Energy Arbitrage
India snapped up deeply discounted Russian crude, slashed import bills, and refined it for re-export at world prices — a textbook margin play.
Strategic Gain
Non-Alignment Dividend
Staying neutral meant India kept trade links with both sanctioning powers and Russia, becoming an indispensable economic bridge.
+7.1% GDP
Domestic Resilience
Consumer demand surged, logistics held firm, and the rupee stayed stable — making India the world's fastest-growing major economy through the crisis.
Strong Growth
